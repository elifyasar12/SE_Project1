Research

Existing Work

My research focuses on deep learning methods for medical image analysis, specifically detecting bone fractures in X-ray images using convolutional neural networks and computer vision techniques. Medical imaging plays a critical role in modern healthcare, and improving the accuracy and efficiency of fracture detection can significantly assist medical professionals in diagnosis and treatment planning. In my work, I explore how neural network architectures can automatically identify fracture patterns within radiographic images while reducing the time required for manual analysis. By leveraging deep learning models and large medical image datasets, it is possible to train systems that learn complex visual features associated with bone fractures. My research also involves evaluating model performance using metrics such as accuracy, precision, and recall to better understand how these systems can support clinical decision-making.

Future Interests

In the future, I am interested in expanding my research toward AI-driven healthcare systems that combine machine learning with real-world medical applications. One of my main interests is interpretable machine learning, which focuses on building models that not only make accurate predictions but also provide explanations that medical professionals can understand and trust. I am particularly interested in developing intelligent diagnostic tools that assist physicians in analyzing complex medical data and identifying potential health conditions earlier.
